# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Smith Match contributors, maintainers, and participants commit to a harassment-free, respectful,
welcoming environment regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity or expression, experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, appearance,
race, caste, color, religion, sexual identity or orientation, or technical preference.

Constructive technical disagreement is welcome. Personal attacks, harassment, threats, stalking,
sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, publication of private information, sustained disruption,
and other conduct reasonably inappropriate for a professional community are not.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, release/community spaces,
and public interactions where someone represents Smith Match. It also applies when conduct outside the
project creates a credible safety or participation concern inside project spaces.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers clarify standards and take fair, proportionate action. They may edit, hide, reject, or remove
comments, commits, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate this policy. They may
issue a correction, warning, temporary restriction, or permanent ban. Decisions should consider impact,
context, repeated behavior, participant safety, and conflicts of interest. Enforcement details remain
as private as the available reporting channel permits.

## Reporting

This repository has no dedicated private maintainer mailbox. Do not send sensitive incident details
through a public issue or pull request.

For sensitive conduct occurring on GitHub, use GitHub's private
[Report Abuse or Spam](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse) channel or the **Report content**
control attached to the relevant content. Those reports go to GitHub, not directly to Smith Match
maintainers; GitHub controls confidentiality, response, and platform enforcement.

For a non-sensitive project moderation request, mention `@wmacomber` in the relevant issue or pull
request without quoting harmful material or private information. Maintainers will handle visible project
content and participation within repository permissions. Immediate danger should go to appropriate local
emergency services.

Security vulnerabilities use the [Security Policy](SECURITY.md), not conduct channels.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers generally use this progression, adjusted for severity and safety:

1. **Correction:** private or public clarification and requested behavior change.
2. **Warning:** documented consequence for continued or repeated conduct.
3. **Temporary restriction:** time-limited removal from project interaction.
4. **Permanent restriction:** indefinite removal for serious, sustained, or repeated violations.

Retaliation against a reporter or participant in an enforcement review is itself a violation.
